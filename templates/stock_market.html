What Are Stocks?

A stock represents a share of ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you become a partial owner of that company and are entitled to a portion of its profits and assets.

Common Stocks

Give voting rights and potential for dividends. Higher risk, higher reward.

Preferred Stocks

No voting rights but fixed dividends. Priority over common stock during liquidation.

Key Insight: Stocks are traded on exchanges like the NYSE and NASDAQ, where prices are determined by supply and demand.

Reading Stock Charts

Candlestick Charts are the most popular among traders.

Bullish Candle (Green): Closing price is higher than Opening price.
Bearish Candle (Red): Closing price is lower than Opening price.

Key Indicators

  • Volume: Number of shares traded. Confirms trends.
  • RSI (Relative Strength Index): Measures momentum (Overbought/Oversold).
  • Moving Averages (MA): Smooths out price action to show trends.

Trading Strategies

Day Trading

Buying and selling within the same day. High risk, requires active monitoring.

Swing Trading

Holding for days or weeks to capture medium-term moves. Technical analysis focus.

Long-term Investing

Holding for years based on company fundamentals (Value Investing).

Risk Management Rule #1: Never risk more than 1-2% of your total portfolio on a single trade. Always use Stop Losses.
